introduction

All things are aggregations of atoms that dance and by their movement produce sound. When the rhythm of the dance changes, the sound it produces also changes. Each atom perpetually sings its song, and the sound at every moment creates dense subtle forms.

-Alexandra David-Neel (1868-1969)

As technology becomes more and more integrated into daily living, accessing music has never been easier. Yet, as the world of music continues to expand at such a rapid pace, there’s a lot that listeners take for granted when listening, downloading, or streaming music on a daily basis. There’s hundreds of years of scientific discovery, technological innovation and human effort that has allowed you to plug in your headphones and listen to your favorite songs wherever and whenever you want to.

While it’s not necessary to understand the nuances of what goes behind producing music to enjoy it, Science in Sound will help you develop an understanding of the process and the technology involved. With this knowledge, you will walk away with a deeper appreciation for the sheer amount of effort that goes behind each and every song you listen to.
